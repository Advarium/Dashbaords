GDACS Global Disasters▾
Global Disaster Alert and Coordination System (EU JRC). Diamond markers labeled with event type (TC, FL, EQ…) and colored by humanitarian alert level. Covers tropical cyclones, floods, earthquakes, droughts, wildfires, volcanoes, and tsunamis worldwide.
Red Alert — Major humanitarian impact expected
Orange Alert — Significant impact expected
Green Alert — Limited impact
Meteoalarm — Europe▾
Active weather warnings for 33 European countries via Meteoalarm / EUMETNET. Polygon fill shown where country provides boundary geometry. Click a polygon or panel item to open warning details.
Minor — Low impact
Moderate — Moderate impact
Severe — High impact
Extreme — Very high impact, threat to life

SPC Day 1 & 2 fire weather outlooks. Filled polygons show categorical risk areas; dashed purple outlines mark Dry Thunderstorm areas (lightning with little rain).

US Drought Monitor▾
Weekly NOAA/UNL Drought Monitor polygons across CONUS. Updated each Thursday; reflects conditions through the previous Tuesday. Polygons show drought intensity.
D0 — Abnormally Dry
Short-term dryness slowing planting or pasture; elevated fire risk above normal
D1 — Moderate Drought
Some crop damage; high fire risk; streams, reservoirs, or wells low
D2 — Severe Drought
Crop/pasture losses likely; water shortages common; restrictions likely
D3 — Extreme Drought
Major crop/pasture losses; widespread water shortages or restrictions
D4 — Exceptional Drought
Exceptional losses; widespread shortages of water in all reported uses

GOES-West IR▾
NOAA GOES-West (GOES-18) ABI Band 13 (10.3 µm) Clean Infrared via NASA GIBS. Covers eastern Pacific, Alaska, and western CONUS. 10-min scan cadence, ~40-min GIBS lag. NASA GIBS renders tiles with a fixed Enhanced IR color table — colors indicate cloud-top brightness temperature, not water vapor content.
Cloud-top Brightness Temperature — Enhanced IR Scale
>−31°C−42°C−52°C−62°C<−70°C
Gray tones — warm surface and low/mid clouds (>−31°C / >242 K)
Cyan — high cold cloud tops (−32 to −42°C)
Blue — very cold tops, deep cirrus (−42 to −52°C)
Green — deep convective towers (−52 to −62°C)
Yellow / Orange — intense storms / hurricane eyewalls (−62 to −70°C)
Red — extreme / overshooting tops (<−70°C / <203 K)
GOES-East IR▾
NOAA GOES-East (GOES-16) ABI Band 13 (10.3 µm) Clean Infrared via NASA GIBS. Covers Americas and the Atlantic Ocean. Identical sensor and Enhanced IR colormap as GOES-West. ~10-min scan cadence, ~40-min GIBS lag.

Cyan — high cold tops (−32 to −42°C)
Blue — very cold tops (−42 to −52°C)
Green — deep convection (−52 to −62°C)
Yellow / Orange — intense storms / Atlantic hurricanes (−62 to −70°C)
Red — extreme / overshooting tops (<−70°C)
Meteosat-11 IR▾
EUMETSAT Meteosat-11 SEVIRI Band 9 (10.8 µm) thermal infrared via SSEC RealEarth. Full-disk coverage of Europe, Africa, and the Indian Ocean. 15-minute scan cadence. RealEarth serves this product as a grayscale image.

Himawari IR▾
JMA Himawari-8/9 AHI Band 13 (10.4 µm) thermal IR via SSEC RealEarth. Full-disk view of East Asia, the western Pacific, and Australia. 10-minute scan cadence. RealEarth serves this product as a grayscale image.
Cloud-top Brightness Temperature (Grayscale)
Warm / surfaceCold / high cloud
Dark = warm surface / low cloud · Light gray = mid-level cloud · White = cold high-altitude tops (typhoon outflow, western Pacific convection)
GRACE Groundwater▾
NASA GRACE-FO Liquid Water Equivalent Thickness — total terrestrial water storage anomaly (groundwater + soil + surface water + snow/ice) relative to the 2004–2009 baseline. Monthly product; ~3-month lag. Via NASA GIBS.
