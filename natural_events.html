🏜️ US Drought Monitor▾
Weekly NOAA/UNL Drought Monitor polygons across CONUS. Updated each Thursday; reflects conditions through the previous Tuesday. Polygons show drought intensity.
D0 — Abnormally Dry
Short-term dryness slowing planting or pasture; elevated fire risk above normal
D1 — Moderate Drought
Some crop damage; high fire risk; streams, reservoirs, or wells low
D2 — Severe Drought
Crop/pasture losses likely; water shortages common; restrictions likely
D3 — Extreme Drought
Major crop/pasture losses; widespread water shortages or restrictions
D4 — Exceptional Drought
Exceptional losses; widespread shortages of water in all reported uses

🛰️ GOES-West IR▾
NOAA GOES-West (GOES-18) ABI Band 13 (10.3 µm) Clean Infrared via NASA GIBS. Covers eastern Pacific, Alaska, and western CONUS. 10-min scan cadence, ~40-min GIBS lag. NASA GIBS renders tiles with a fixed Enhanced IR color table — colors indicate cloud-top brightness temperature, not water vapor content.
Cloud-top Brightness Temperature — Enhanced IR Scale
>−31°C−42°C−52°C−62°C<−70°C
Gray tones — warm surface and low/mid clouds (>−31°C / >242 K)
Cyan — high cold cloud tops (−32 to −42°C)
Blue — very cold tops, deep cirrus (−42 to −52°C)
Green — deep convective towers (−52 to −62°C)
Yellow / Orange — intense storms / hurricane eyewalls (−62 to −70°C)
Red — extreme / overshooting tops (<−70°C / <203 K)
